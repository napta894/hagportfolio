Lotus app is a digital solution for users
that are ingredient-conscious as they often find it challenging to find a truly organic product and they lack time to check ingredients labels.

The app's main features are a scanner to check organic products ingredients and shopping online to find an organic products to compare with your own.


Role

UX/UI designer

Duration

10 Weeks

Tools

Invision

Problem space

Health and environmentally conscious people want to use cosmetic products that are truly organic. However, they find it very difficult to find such products as they can not understand and are often misled by organic cosmetic products labels.

Solution

An app that allow users to scan organic ingredients products, read quickly ingredients lists and find an organic product to compare with their own.

Design process

Method: Double diamond

Primary research

In this step, I created questions to understand users' behaviour,
motivation and pain points in terms of misleading brand experiences and how they interact when buying an organic product.

Hypothesis statement

I believe that healthy and ingredient-conscious people are misled when buying organic products
I will know if I am right or wrong when I see the following feedback from the market: participants mistrusting labels and secondary data concerning mistrusting labels.

To confirm my hypothesis I interviewed 4 users from 20-55 that are
health and environmentally conscious. Here what they said:

“I do not believe brands when they say is natural because sometimes
people should be careful about allergens.”

“It is very difficult to find organic products”

“Sometimes I feel overwhelmed and confused
when I buy organic products online””

Key Findings

Difficulty trusting
brands

Users finds difficult to trust brands as “Greenwashing”
it is relatively common.

Online shopping experience

When buying online
users find it hard to explore organic product options

Transparent and descriptive
Ingredient labels

Users find it difficult to read the ingredients labels
and they do not have time to read them all.

Chosen Theme

Transparent and descriptive ingredient labels

I have chosen this theme as the main one because it has major pain points, behavior and motivations. When buying organic cosmetic products users want to see clearly and upfront what they use.

Task flow selection

Referencing the user mapping, the user stories and epics.
The selected task flow is searching for an organic cosmetic product because is a crucial task for our persona.

Scenario

Laura searches and selects a face moisturizer from the search result page. She reads the product's ingredients and adds the product to the cart. She scans a product to compare it with the one added to the cart. Finally, she orders the discovered face moisturizer.

Usability testing

The initial prototype will be used to conduct a usability testing.
Two rounds of usability testing were conducted with 5 testers to improve the design and find usability issues.

Round 1

The tasks were simple to undertake, however most of testers were expecting more on the ingredient details as well on the scanning product page.

Round 2

Overall the tasks was completed from most of the user except for the second task (scanning). This round was quite different than the previous one as I did not iterate the prototyping of the bottom navigation.

Key issues: Round 1

1. Going back is not obvious in all the screens
2. The stars on the products it does’nt show what is the number
3. Listing of the ingredients is confusing
4. Toxicity meter is not explaining its purpose

Key issues: Round 2

5. Difficulty to click on scanning as the scan icon is not cliccable
6. Organic percentage description on the search results is not readable
7. At the ingredient lists page the arrow to go back is not cliccable.
8. At the product page the view link needs to be more visible as it is the main purpose of the app
